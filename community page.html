    WATER WASTAGE

    Water Wastage refers to the unneccessary or inefficient use of water which leads to the depletion of this valuable/precious resource. It encompasses a wide range of activities and practices, both at the individual and social levels, that result in the squandering of water without providing commensurate benefits. Water wastage is a pressing global concern due to the finite nature of freshwater resources and their critical role in sustaining life, ecosystems, and various industries. Efforts to reduce water wastage are essential for addressing water scarcity, conserving resources, and promoting sustainability. Read more

    GOVERNMENT INITIATIVE

  • Jal Shakti Abhiyan
    • Launched in 2019 by the Indian government, 
the Jal Shakti Abhiyan aims to accelerate 
water conservation efforts across the country. 
This water conservation schemeâ€™s key strategies
include water conservation, rainwater harvesting,
renovation of traditional water bodies, and 
watershed development.

  • Atal Bhujal Yojana
    • The Atal Bhujal Yojana,
an initiative by the Ministry of Jal Shakti,
emphasizes sustainable groundwater management.
The scheme is centered on participatory 
groundwater management, involving Panchayat Raj
Institutions and local stakeholders.

  • National Water Mission
    • The National Water Mission, under
the National Action Plan on Climate Change,
targets comprehensive water management 
focusing on surface and groundwater resources,
demand management, and governance structure.
